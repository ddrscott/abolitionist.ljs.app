# If You Oppose Abortion But You Are Not a Christian

## You are likely here if you believe

- Abortion is wrong — you don't need a Bible to tell you that.
- You want to support abolition efforts, financially or otherwise.
- Religious disagreements shouldn't block cooperation on a clear moral issue.
- You are a Roman Catholic, Muslim, atheist, agnostic, Mormon, or Jehovah's Witness, and your tradition or your conscience opposes abortion.
- You might donate to abolitionist organizations, attend rallies, or vote for abolitionist candidates.

## The question your position cannot answer

The movement you are considering joining is not primarily a coalition against a policy. Its stated measure of success is "faithful obedience to the Lord Jesus Christ" — not lives saved, not bills passed, not hearts changed. You can share the conclusion (abortion is wrong) without sharing the premises or the aim. That gap is not a minor preference about strategy. It changes what the movement *is*. The corpus names it plainly: "we are at war with EVERY. SINGLE. WORLDVIEW. that has been set up against the knowledge of Christ."

If you know that abortion is wrong, you are responding to God's law written on the conscience (Romans 2:14–15). That the conscience accuses you regarding this specific sin does not make the conscience reliable on every matter. The same Scripture that teaches the wrongness of murder teaches that no one is saved by opposing murder — salvation is in Christ alone, by faith alone, through grace alone. Opposition to abortion cannot justify you before God. Only Christ can.

## Read in this order

1. **[FAQ — "Can People Who Disagree With You Join The Abolitionist Movement?"](/docs/abolitionistsrising.com/faq)** — This answer in §1 is written directly to you. It names your position by category (Roman Catholic, Muslim, atheist, agnostic, Mormon, Jehovah's Witness), explains why the movement cannot recognize you as an abolitionist, and makes a plain call. Read it in full before proceeding.
2. **[Norman Statement](/docs/abolitionistsrising.com/norman-statement)** — the doctrinal statement of the movement. Read not as a list of political principles, but as a confession of Christ. Article XI in particular is the gospel you are being called toward — not to a platform, but to a Savior.
3. **[FAQ — "Since not everyone is a Christian, shouldn't we argue from a secular perspective?"](/docs/abolitionistsrising.com/faq)** — also in §1. The Garrison quote lives here: "Take away the Bible, and our warfare with oppression… is at an end." The movement does not believe it can win this fight without the only weapon that addresses the root.
4. **[Abolitionist Theological Foundations](/docs/abolitionistsrising.com/theology)** — especially the sections on the gospel being the cure and the Church possessing that cure. The movement holds that sin — including the sin of abortion — is addressed only at the cross of Christ. Read this to understand what you are being called toward.

## The specific objection the corpus answers

> "We have the same goal. Why can't we partner?"

Your stated goal is the abolition of abortion. The movement's stated goal is "the abolition of abortion *to the glory of God*." These are not the same goal. Shared ends do not create shared means when the means is obedience to Christ. The corpus is explicit: anti-abortion unbelievers are part of the movement's mission field, not its membership. **Read [the full FAQ answer](/docs/abolitionistsrising.com/faq) on this point** — the movement is transparent about why it will not treat you as a partner even while it welcomes your opposition to abortion and prays for your repentance.

## When you've read these

You are not being told your opposition to abortion is worthless. You are being told it is not enough — not for ending abortion, and more importantly, not for you.

This page is deliberately withholding the next-steps link that closes every other path on this site. The reason is simple: the next step for you is not a signature or a rally. It is repentance and faith in Jesus Christ.

The archive puts it this way:

> "Dear reader, if this description fits you, or you find your spirit rising up in protest against what we are saying here, we urge you to hear us on this: you must repent of your sin, turn to Christ, and put your faith and trust in Jesus Christ alone for your salvation."

Read the [Norman Statement](/docs/abolitionistsrising.com/norman-statement) again — not as a political observer, but as someone being called. Then find a Bible-preaching church near you. Tell them you are coming because you have read that you need Jesus Christ. If you don't know where to start, people in an abolitionist church will know.

When you have come to faith in Christ, the rest of this site is waiting for you. Until then, these articles are what you need.
